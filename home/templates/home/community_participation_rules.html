Outreachy community sign up

Outreachy welcomes open source projects, open science projects, and non-profits who wish to mentor Outreachy interns.

For Outreachy, the word community means an open source project, open science project, or non-profit.

Please review the rules below before signing up your community.

Eligibility rules

1. Mentoring communities must be open source, open science, or a non-profit

Three types of communities can participate as an Outreachy mentoring community:

  • open source communities
  • open science communities
  • non-profit organizations

2. Internship projects must be published in an open, public manner

All work done by the intern must be publicly released under an open source license.

3. No proprietary software

Outreachy intern projects must not be tied to proprietary software.

Common mistakes

Interns are not contractors

Outreachy interns are not contractors for hire. You cannot hire an Outreachy intern to work on a project you are not experienced in.

You should not expect an Outreachy intern to complete their project. Outreachy's goal is to teach interns about open source, not to get a particular project completed.

Interns are not employees

Outreachy interns are not employees of any Outreachy sponsor. Outreachy does not "place" interns with companies.
